OpenRISC is an open ISA that has been around for twenty years now. It has a very open, friendly community around hobbyists and has seen some commercial adoption. There is one well-maintained reference open source implementation, mor1kx, and the community is constantly working on getting all tools upstream and keep them updated.

The OpenRISC has been the incubator for ORConf (the largest open source digital design conference), FOSSi Foundation (a non-profit foundation that supports the open source silicon community), and librecores.org (a community hub for open source silicon IP).

Alternatives exists, open and proprietary, and you should obviously decide which ISA fits your needs. You also can have more than one favorite ISA.
